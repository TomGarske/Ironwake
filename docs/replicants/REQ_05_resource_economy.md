# REQ_05: Resource Economy
**Replicants: Swarm Command**

## Resource Model Overview

The **Metal** economy is the lifeblood of the swarm. It drives replication, fuels protocols, and creates strategic pressure. Scarcity is intentional—players must expand to survive.

---

## Primary Resource: Metal

### Definition
Metal is a **harvested material** found in facility deposits. It is the **only primary resource** in Replicants. (Secondary: swarm unit count, but this is produced, not harvested.)

### Mechanics
- **Harvested by:** Harvester units from MetalDeposit nodes.
- **Consumed by:**
  - Replication (unit production at ReplicationHub).
  - Protocol commands (Rapid Replication, Assimilation Wave).
- **Generated by:** Assimilators converting resistance structures.
- **Capacity:** No hard cap, but depiction shows counter (current/max reserve for display purposes).

### Economy State
```
Total Metal =
  (Metal Harvested) - (Metal Spent on Replication) - (Metal Spent on Protocols) + (Metal Assimilated from Structures)
```

---

## Metal Deposits

Deposits are resource nodes scattered across the facility map. Each has distinct characteristics.

### Deposit Types

| Type | Size | Yield | Initial Amount | Harvest Rate | Resistance Presence | Respawn |
|------|------|-------|-----------------|--------------|---------------------|---------|
| **Small** | 20px | Quick | 40 metal | 1 metal/sec | None | No |
| **Medium** | 32px | Moderate | 100 metal | 0.8 metal/sec | Optional guard | No |
| **Large** | 48px | High-Value | 250 metal | 1 metal/sec | Guaranteed (Turret + Patrol) | No |

### Deposit Depletion
- Deposits deplete continuously as Harvesters extract.
- Once depleted, deposit **disappears from map**.
- No respawning; players must find new deposits to expand.
- **Strategic Implication:** Scarcity drives expansion. Finding new deposits = discovering new resistance.

### Deposit Discovery
- Deposits are revealed by **Scout units** as they move through fog of war.
- Once revealed, deposits appear on minimap with a **diamond icon**.
- Large deposits are marked with a **warning icon** (indicates heavy resistance).

### Harvesting Mechanic
1. Harvester approaches deposit (within 30px).
2. Harvester enters deposit's **Area2D** and begins extraction.
3. Metal accumulates at **harvest_rate** per second.
4. Harvested metal is **immediately added to shared pool** (no inventory carriage).
5. If Harvester is damaged or commanded away, harvesting pauses (does not cancel).
6. Harvester can resume harvesting the same deposit if returns within 5 seconds (warm start).

## Replication Economy

### Replication Costs (Metal per Unit)

| Unit Type | AWAKENING | EARLY_COLONY | EXPANSION | RESISTANCE_SURGE |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|-----------|------------------|
| Harvester | 8 | 8 | 12 | 18 |
| Scout | 5 | 5 | 8 | 12 |
| Soldier | 12 | 12 | 18 | 25 |
| Builder | 15 | 15 | 22 | 30 |
| Assimilator | — | — | 20 | 15 |

### Cost Scaling Logic
- **AWAKENING:** Base costs. Economy is abundant (tutorial metal deposit is large).
- **EARLY_COLONY:** Costs remain base, but deposits are smaller. First scarcity pressure.
- **EXPANSION:** Costs increase 50%. Swarms grow, but resources tighten. Strategic unit choice becomes critical.
- **RESISTANCE_SURGE:** Costs spike 100%+. Assimilator cost drops (incentivizing late-game conversion strategy).

### Production Mechanics
- **ReplicationHub** is the production facility.
- **Queue:** Up to 5 units can be queued per hub.
- **Production Time:** 6 seconds per unit (base). Affected by Rapid Replication protocol (×2 speed).
- **Metal Deduction:** Metal is **deducted upfront** when unit is queued, not at spawn.
- **Queue Processing:** FIFO. Units are produced one at a time, in order.

### Example Economy Sequence (EARLY_COLONY)
```
Time 0:
  Metal: 50
  Queue a Harvester (cost 8) → Metal: 42
  Queue a Soldier (cost 12) → Metal: 30

Time 6:
  Harvester spawns, production begins next unit (Soldier)
  Harvesters are harvesting nearby deposit → +1 metal/sec

Time 12:
  Soldier spawns
  Metal: 36 (accumulated +6 from harvesting, spent 12 on soldier)

Time 18:
  Queue another Harvester (cost 8) → Metal: 28
  Next Harvester begins production (6 sec)

Time 24:
  New Harvester spawns → Metal: 30 (harvesting rate +1 metal/sec)
```

### Metal Income
- **Per Harvester:** 1 metal/sec (on Small/Medium deposit), 1 metal/sec (on Large).
- **Total Income:** (# of Harvesters) × (harvest rate of assigned deposit).
- **Assimilation Income:** Assimilators generate metal by converting resistance structures (5 metal/sec during assimilation, 10 metal/sec during Assimilation Wave protocol).

### Economy Pressure Curve
```
Metal Income (metal/sec)
      |
   10 |                    ╱─ Assimilation surge (Wave protocol)
      |            ╱─────╱
    5 |      ╱────╱
      |  ╱──╱
    0 |──────────────────────
      0       5      10      15    (Time in minutes)
      EARLY_COLONY → EXPANSION → RESISTANCE_SURGE
```

## Protocol Command Costs

### Swarm Rush
- **Metal Cost:** 0 (free).
- **Unit Cost:** 1 Soldier (per attack? No; command duration is time-based).
- **Cooldown:** 20 seconds.
- **Strategic Use:** Concentrate force on critical target (Turret, Commander).

### Rapid Replication
- **Metal Cost:** 20 metal (deducted upfront).
- **Effect:** Double production speed for 15 seconds.
- **Cooldown:** 45 seconds.
- **Strategic Use:** Bulk produce Soldiers in response to escalation, or Harvesters if deposits found.

### Scatter
- **Metal Cost:** 0 (free).
- **Cooldown:** 8 seconds (very fast recharge).
- **Strategic Use:** Panic button to avoid area attacks (EMP bursts, Turret sweeps).

### Defensive Formation
- **Metal Cost:** 0 (free).
- **Cooldown:** 25 seconds.
- **Strategic Use:** Protect critical positions (Replication Hub, chokepoint).

### Assimilation Wave
- **Metal Cost:** 0 (but sacrifices 1 Assimilator per target).
- **Cooldown:** 30 seconds.
- **Strategic Use:** Convert enemy defenses into metal income (high-value late-game).

## Multiplayer Economy (Shared Pool)

### Shared Metal Tracking
- All players contribute to and draw from the **same metal pool**.
- Any player can queue units (all queued units deduct from shared pool, FIFO).
- If insufficient metal, queue waits until metal is available.

### Contribution Tracking
- **Per-player stats:** Track how much metal each player harvested (for end-game leaderboard).
- **UI Display:** HUD shows "[Player A: 120 metal harvested] [Player B: 95 harvested]" at end of mission.

### Command Conflict on Queuing
- If Player A and Player B queue units simultaneously:
  - Commands are processed in **timestamp order** (first queue wins).
  - If metal is insufficient after Player A's queue, Player B's queue waits.
  - UI shows a **queue status** (e.g., "Queued: Soldier #2, waiting for metal").

### Replication Hub Ownership
- Hubs created by Player A are not "owned" exclusively; any player can queue units at any hub.
- This encourages cooperation and shared resource management.

## Economic Phases by Mission State

### AWAKENING
- **Metal Abundance:** Tutorial deposit is large (no depletion).
- **Income:** ~1–2 metal/sec from single Harvester.
- **Pressure:** Minimal. Focus on learning mechanics.
- **Deposits:** 1 non-depleting tutorial deposit.

### EARLY_COLONY
- **Metal Scarcity:** Deposits are Small/Medium; deplete within 3–5 minutes.
- **Income:** 2–4 metal/sec (2–4 Harvesters on varying deposits).
- **Pressure:** High. Must find new deposits to sustain growth.
- **Deposits:** 2–3 small deposits (one safe, one guarded, one hidden).

### EXPANSION
- **Metal Dynamics:** Multiple deposits available, but costs increase 50%.
- **Income:** 5–8 metal/sec (5–8 Harvesters, or fewer on high-yield large deposits).
- **Pressure:** Balanced. Players must choose: expand swarm or defend territory.
- **Deposits:** 4–5 medium/large deposits (mostly guarded by resistance).
- **Replication Cost Impact:** Cost spike forces players to specialize unit choices.

### RESISTANCE_SURGE
- **Metal Scarcity:** Harvesting is disrupted (enemy interference reduces effectiveness by 30%).
- **Income:** 3–5 metal/sec (disrupted harvesting).
- **Pressure:** Extreme. Metal is the bottleneck. Assimilation becomes critical.
- **Deposits:** Few new deposits; mostly guarded heavily.
- **Assimilation Compensation:** Assimilators generate 5–10 metal/sec (incentive to convert enemy structures).

---

## Economic Victory Condition Linkage

### Assimilation Requires Sustained Economy
- **20% Assimilation (EARLY_COLONY):** Requires ~80–120 metal spent on swarm growth (8–12 Harvesters → Soldiers → defend).
- **50% Assimilation (EXPANSION):** Requires ~300–500 metal (larger swarm, more protocols).
- **Neutralize Commanders (RESISTANCE_SURGE):** Requires concentrated Soldier force (100+ metal on soldiers) + Assimilators to break through (80+ metal).

### Economic Failure = Mission Failure
- If metal income drops to 0 (all Harvesters dead, all deposits depleted), swarm cannot grow.
- Without growth, swarm cannot overwhelm resistance.
- Without metal for Rapid Replication or Assimilation Wave, players cannot respond to escalation.
- **Result:** Defeat by attrition.

## GDScript Implementation

### MetalEconomy Manager
```gdscript
# MetalEconomy.gd
class_name MetalEconomy
extends Node

@export var starting_metal: int = 50
@export var max_reserve: int = 500

var current_metal: int = 0
var metal_per_second: float = 0.0
var total_harvested: int = 0
var total_spent: int = 0

var active_harvesters: Array[SwarmUnit] = []

signal metal_changed(new_amount: int)
signal metal_depleted()
signal harvest_rate_changed(rate: float)

func _ready() -> void:
	current_metal = starting_metal
	metal_changed.emit(current_metal)

func _process(delta: float) -> void:
	_update_harvesting(delta)

func _update_harvesting(delta: float) -> void:
	var harvest_rate = _calculate_harvest_rate()
	metal_per_second = harvest_rate
	harvest_rate_changed.emit(harvest_rate)

	var metal_gained = int(harvest_rate * delta)
	if metal_gained > 0:
		current_metal += metal_gained
		total_harvested += metal_gained
		metal_changed.emit(current_metal)

func _calculate_harvest_rate() -> float:
	var rate = 0.0
	for harvester in active_harvesters:
		if harvester and not harvester.is_queued_for_deletion():
			# Check if harvester is in a deposit
			var deposit = _get_harvester_deposit(harvester)
			if deposit:
				rate += deposit.harvest_rate
	return rate

func spend_metal(amount: int) -> bool:
	if current_metal >= amount:
		current_metal -= amount
		total_spent += amount
		metal_changed.emit(current_metal)
		return true
	else:
		return false

func register_harvester(harvester: SwarmUnit) -> void:
	if harvester not in active_harvesters:
		active_harvesters.append(harvester)
		harvester.died.connect(_on_harvester_died.bind(harvester))

func _on_harvester_died(harvester: SwarmUnit) -> void:
	active_harvesters.erase(harvester)
	harvest_rate_changed.emit(_calculate_harvest_rate())

func _get_harvester_deposit(harvester: SwarmUnit) -> MetalDeposit:
	var overlapping_areas = harvester.get_overlapping_areas()
	for area in overlapping_areas:
		if area is MetalDeposit:
			return area
	return null
```

	pass

func deplete() -> void:
	is_depleted = true
	remaining_metal = 0
	depleted.emit()
	_update_visual()
	queue_free()  # Remove from map once depleted

func _update_visual() -> void:
	if is_depleted:
		modulate = Color.DIM_GRAY
		$Sprite2D.self_modulate = Color.DIM_GRAY
	else:
		var depletion_ratio = float(remaining_metal) / initial_amount
		var color = Color.WHITE.lerp(Color.DARK_GRAY, 1.0 - depletion_ratio)
		modulate = color

### ReplicationQueue (Hub Production)
```gdscript
# ReplicationQueue.gd
class_name ReplicationQueue
extends Node

var hub: ReplicationHub
var economy: MetalEconomy
var queue: Array[String] = []
var current_unit: String = ""
var production_timer: float = 0.0

const BASE_PRODUCTION_TIME = 6.0
const UNIT_COSTS = {
	"harvester": 8,
	"scout": 5,
	"soldier": 12,
	"builder": 15,
	"assimilator": 20
}

signal unit_queued(unit_type: String, queue_size: int)
signal production_started(unit_type: String)
signal unit_spawned(unit_type: String)

func queue_unit(unit_type: String) -> bool:
	if queue.size() >= 5:
		return false  # Queue full

	var cost = UNIT_COSTS.get(unit_type, 0)
	if economy.spend_metal(cost):
		queue.append(unit_type)
		unit_queued.emit(unit_type, queue.size())
		if current_unit.is_empty():
			_start_production()
		return true
	return false

func _start_production() -> void:
	if queue.is_empty():
		return

	current_unit = queue.pop_front()
	production_timer = BASE_PRODUCTION_TIME
	production_started.emit(current_unit)

func _process(delta: float) -> void:
	if not current_unit.is_empty():
		production_timer -= delta
		if production_timer <= 0.0:
			hub._spawn_unit(current_unit)
			unit_spawned.emit(current_unit)
			current_unit = ""
			_start_production()

func get_queue_status() -> String:
	if current_unit.is_empty() and queue.is_empty():
		return "Idle"
	elif current_unit.is_empty():
		return "Waiting for metal..."
	else:
		var time_remaining = int(production_timer)
		return "%s (%ds)" % [current_unit, time_remaining]
```

## Economy Balancing Considerations

### Early Game (AWAKENING)
- Tutorial deposit should provide 50+ metal with minimal loss.
- Single Harvester should sustain 1 Soldier + 1 Harvester production indefinitely.

### Mid Game (EARLY_COLONY → EXPANSION)
- Players should be able to sustain 4–6 Harvesters + 8–12 Soldiers with careful expansion.
- Cost spike at EXPANSION should force players to choose between aggressive growth and defensive stability.

### Late Game (RESISTANCE_SURGE)
- Metal income should feel **scarce** (3–5 metal/sec).
- Assimilation should feel **rewarding** (5–10 metal/sec).
- Players should feel the pressure to convert enemy structures instead of harvesting.

### Testing Metrics
- Time to accumulate 50 metal (should be 25–40 seconds in EARLY_COLONY with 2 Harvesters).
- Time to produce first 10 Soldiers (should be 2–3 minutes with sustainable economy).
- Time to reach 50% assimilation (should be 10–15 minutes in EXPANSION).

---

## Notes

- **No inflation:** Metal count should remain tractable (max ~500 at any point). Avoid exponential growth.
- **Scarcity is intentional:** If economy feels too abundant, remove deposits or increase costs.
- **Assimilation incentive:** Late game should reward conversion play. Assimilator cost drop and income boost reinforce this.
